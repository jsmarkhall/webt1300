Introduction Bands Recommended Songs

1. Architects

(© Ed Mason Photography)

At the top of the list I'd like to introduce Architects. Architects are a british group that recently released "For Those That Wish to Exist," an almost hour-long album that explores a sound that does more than build off of the foundation of previous works. Prior to this album they had a much more defined sound that people came to know them for. Filled with ambient guitars, heavy riffs, and complex rhythm over all, Architects used a formula up until this new album. So in terms of the main album that is considered a true, metalcore masterpiece, their 2016 work "All Our Gods Have Abandoned Us" really drives the genre home with it's incredible songwriting and guitar work by the late Tom Searle, who wrote what most consider to be their best works. It's considered that this album moved the entire genre in a new direction, making it one of the most important albums of the genre.

2. Bring Me the Horizon

(© Justin Borucki Photography)

Second on the list is another british group known as Bring Me the Horizon. Sempiternal, released in 2013, serves as a landmark in the genre as songs like "Can You Feel My Heart," "Shadow Moses" and "Sleepwalking" made it into the world of mainstream music bringing more attention to the genre. Oddly enough, over time, Bring Me the Horizon would take a different direction with their music that was more pop-based that was heavily present in amo (2019), and then throwing a curveball with their most recent EP, Post Human: Survival Horror which was more metalcore based and even contained a song featuring mainstream artist YUNGBLUD. This is important because it just shows that Bring me the Horizon has the ability to do more than what they became known for, and that's why they are on this list. They are very talented and their contributions to the mental health community through releases such as Sempiternal, which had very emotional songwriting that was considered highly relatable have to be recognized.

3. The Amity Affliction

© Credit Unknown)

Finally, I feel like the Amity Affliction deserves some recognition on this list. They have a discography that is hard to argue with, filled with heavy instrumentals and powerful vocals/lyrics that some would say make metalcore metalcore. An album such as Let the Ocean Take Me (2014) is such an iconic album as it featured hit songs such as "Pittsburgh" and "Don't Lean on Me" but carried the same overall theme/sound throughout the whole album. The Amity Affliction is known for having lyrical content that is relatable with the mental health community, and that's a very important aspect not just for metalcore, but modern music as a whole. Let the Ocean Take Me has all of that and more, and they've continued to create music that has just that making them one of my top reccomendations for people who are interested in the genre.
